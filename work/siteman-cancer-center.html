Project Inheritance & Infrastructure Modernization

Seamless Agency Transition

Inherited Project Management: Successfully inherited and took over maintenance of the Siteman Cancer Center website from a different agency in winter 2025, ensuring continuity of service and maintaining critical functionality for cancer patients and their families during the transition period.

Hosting Infrastructure Upgrade

On-Premises to Pantheon Migration: Migrated the site from on-premises hosting (managed by Washington University) to Pantheon hosting, providing numerous benefits:

  • Enhanced Management: Easier site management for both the client and development team
  • Development Environments: Access to Pantheon's DEV/TEST/LIVE environment structure plus multi-dev capabilities
  • Improved Access: Streamlined database and file access for maintenance and development
  • Security Enhancement: Managed Web Application Firewall (WAF) for improved security
  • Performance Optimization: Better caching and performance optimization tools

Specialized Content Development

Pediatric Cancer Content Architecture

Children's Content Templates: Developed a comprehensive set of specialized templates specifically designed for pediatric cancer content, addressing the unique needs of families dealing with childhood cancer:

  • Children's Landing Page: Dedicated entry point optimized for pediatric cancer information
  • Children's Cancer Content Pages: Specialized templates for age-appropriate cancer information
  • Children's Cancer Types Taxonomy: Organized classification system for different pediatric cancer types
  • Children's Search Page: Specialized search functionality for pediatric-specific content
  • 10+ Reusable Components: Children-specific components designed for flexibility and consistency

Dynamic Content Enhancement

Improved Editor Experience: Significantly enhanced the content editor experience by making previously manual processes dynamic and automated:

  • Podcast Integration: Automated podcast content integration eliminating manual entry
  • Cancer Programs: Dynamic pulling of cancer program information
  • Research Integration: Automated research content updates and display

Current Impact & Future Vision

Immediate Improvements: Since inheriting the project, I've made significant improvements to the hosting infrastructure, content management experience, and specialized pediatric content offering. The migration to Pantheon hosting has improved site reliability and management capabilities.

Comprehensive Website Redesign (2026 Launch)

Full WordPress Rebuild: Currently leading consultation and development on a brand new WordPress site that will be built over the course of the next year for a mid-2026 launch. This comprehensive redesign focuses on creating a patient-centered experience that addresses the diverse needs of individuals at different stages of their cancer journey.

Patient-Centered Design Philosophy

Personalized Cancer Journey: The new site is being designed to help patients easily identify their specific needs and access appropriate care regardless of where they are in their individual cancer journey. Key focus areas include:

  • Journey-Based Navigation: Intuitive pathways based on diagnosis stage and treatment phase
  • Personalized Content: Relevant information surfaced based on cancer type and patient needs
  • Resource Accessibility: Easy access to support services, clinical trials, and educational materials
  • Family Support: Dedicated resources for families and caregivers
